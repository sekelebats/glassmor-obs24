  • Why I stopped using analytics on my personal site 24 May 2026

    Tracking every click started to feel like performing. Removing numbers gave me back the joy of writing without an audience in mind. I wrote about this journey on my old blog.

    Now I write for myself first. If someone reads it, that's a bonus — not a metric to optimize.

  • The tools I actually use daily (2026 edition) 18 May 2026

    A brutally honest list. No sponsors, no affiliate links—just the apps and scripts that survived my relentless testing.

    Here's the raw list:

    • Editor: nvim with minimal plugins
    • Browser: Firefox with containers
    • Notes: Plain markdown in a folder

    And the core philosophy:

    Use tools that disappear. If you notice the tool more than the work, it's not the right tool.

  • Typography is not a detail 10 May 2026

    Good type won't save bad content, but bad type will absolutely ruin good content. Some notes from years of staring at letters.

    Here's a quick CSS snippet I often use:

    body {
  font-family: system-ui, sans-serif;
  line-height: 1.6;
  max-width: 600px;
  margin: 0 auto;
}

    It's not fancy, but it gets the job done.
